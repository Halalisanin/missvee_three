# Miss Vee Perfume — About Copy (Concept Three)

## Brand Voice

Meo Fusciuni-inspired: poetic, intimate, literary. First-person narration. Speaks like a journal entry, not a marketing brochure. Avoids industry jargon (sillage, projection, etc). Uses sensory, emotional language.

---

## The Beginning

Miss Vee Perfume began in a small kitchen in Ekurhuleni, Gauteng — not in a laboratory. I was blending oils the way my grandmother taught me: by instinct, by memory, by closing my eyes and remembering how something smelled and what it meant.

I never set out to start a fragrance brand. I set out to capture moments. The first scent I ever made was an attempt to bottle the smell of my grandfather's study — old books, tobacco, wood polish, and the particular stillness of a room where someone had been thinking deeply. When I gave it to my mother, she cried. She said, "That is exactly him."

That is when I understood that fragrance is not decoration. It is time travel. It is the closest thing we have to walking back into a memory.

## Today

Miss Vee Perfume remains exactly what it was in that kitchen: small, intentional, deeply personal. Every batch is blended by hand. Every bottle is filled, labelled, and packed by me. I do not chase trends. I chase feelings — the weight of morning light, the smell of rain on a tin roof, the warmth of a hand you miss.
