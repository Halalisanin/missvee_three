The Story

Miss Vee Perfume is not a brand. It is a practice of remembering through scent.

The Beginning

Miss Vee Perfume started in a small kitchen in Ekurhuleni, Gauteng. Not in a lab. I was blending oils the way my grandmother taught me: by instinct, by memory, by closing my eyes and trying to remember how something smelled and what it meant.

I never planned to start a fragrance brand. I just wanted to hold onto certain moments. The first scent I ever made was an attempt to bottle the smell of my grandfather's study. Old books, tobacco, wood polish, and the particular stillness of a room where someone had been thinking deeply. When I gave it to my mother, she cried. She said, "That is exactly him."

That is when I realized a fragrance is not decoration. It is the closest thing we have to walking back into a memory.

"A fragrance is not a thing you wear. It is a place you return to. A corner of memory you can carry in your skin."

Today, Miss Vee Perfume is still what it was in that kitchen. Small. Intentional. Personal. Every batch is blended by hand. Every bottle is filled, labelled, and packed by me. I do not chase trends. I chase feelings. The weight of morning light. The smell of rain on a tin roof. The warmth of a hand you miss.

Thank you for letting my memories become part of yours.

Miss Vee

"I do not create fragrances. I remember them. My job is simply to listen closely enough to what a scent wants to say."
